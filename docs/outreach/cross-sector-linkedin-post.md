I've spent the last several weeks interviewing patients, caregivers, nurses, drivers, and veteran advocates about a problem that costs the US healthcare system billions each year:

**People miss medical appointments because transportation coordination fails.**

8 conversations across 5 different groups. Same pattern every time:

**What I found:**
The issue isn't that transportation doesn't exist. It's that no one has a single view of what's happening — the patient, the driver, the clinic, and the family are all operating in the dark. When a ride falls through, by the time anyone finds out, the appointment is already missed.

This affects more than just healthcare. It's an operations problem, a communications problem, and a logistics problem rolled into one.

**How this connects across sectors:**
- **HR:** This is why employees miss work to drive family members to appointments
- **IT/Engineering:** Fragmented systems with no shared data layer — exactly the type of problem a coordination platform can solve
- **Finance:** Missed appointments = lost revenue, readmission penalties, wasted resources
- **Government:** Medicaid spends millions on transportation with limited visibility into outcomes
- **Healthcare:** Staff burnout from manually coordinating rides that should be automated
- **Marketing/Education/Law:** You all work with populations affected by access barriers — even if it shows up differently

**What I'm building:**
CarePath — a coordination layer that gives everyone involved in a medical ride real-time visibility. Not another ride app. A shared timeline connecting the people who arrange rides, the people who drive them, and the people who need them.
