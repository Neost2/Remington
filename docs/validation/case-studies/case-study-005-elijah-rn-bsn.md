# CarePath Case Study #5

## Elijah (Registered Nurse, BSN)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: Registered Nurse (BSN)
- Setting: Healthcare professional with direct patient-care exposure
- Context: Observes appointment adherence and barriers across primary and specialty care
- Segment relevance: Clinical stakeholder with repeated visibility into transportation-related no-shows

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Direct validation interview during founder outreach
- Selection rationale: Frontline healthcare perspective on causes of missed appointments
- Prompt: Asked whether transportation was a primary barrier to primary and specialty care attendance

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quotes from participant:

- "Yes. Throughout my healthcare experience, I have seen patients miss appointments due to transportation barriers."
- "Common reasons include vehicle breakdowns, lack of reliable transportation, financial limitations related to fuel or transportation costs, dependence on family or friends for rides, and communication issues regarding transportation arrangements."
- "Transportation can be a significant barrier to accessing both primary and specialty care, especially for elderly patients, individuals with chronic illnesses, and those living in rural communities."

Core issues identified:

- Reliability failures (vehicle breakdowns and unstable ride options)
- Affordability barriers (fuel and transportation costs)
- Dependency risk (family and friends with changing availability)
- Communication failures in transportation arrangements

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Clinical impact: Delayed treatment and missed follow-up appointments
- Medication impact: Disruptions to medication management continuity
- Outcome impact: Poorer health outcomes when access is delayed
- Access equity impact: Elevated risk in vulnerable populations, including elderly and chronically ill patients

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Participant provided clear confirmation that transportation is a significant barrier in real-world care delivery
- Feedback supports a solution focused on reliability, communication, and accessibility
- Adoption signal: Strong validation of the problem-solution fit from a clinical perspective

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation barriers affect both primary and specialty care pathways
- Medication management is a downstream consequence of transportation disruption
- Clinical professionals recognize transportation as a root cause of poorer outcomes
- Multi-factor barriers (reliability, cost, communication, dependency) must be addressed together

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong

Justification:

- Direct testimony from a frontline RN with broad healthcare exposure
- Specific barrier categories and patient populations identified
- Explicit link between transportation failures and poorer health outcomes

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

Pilot priorities reinforced by this case:

- Keep reliability and communication as top product priorities
- Add workflow support for recurring follow-up and medication-related visits
- Prioritize vulnerable populations in risk scoring and alerting
- Design affordability-aware and fallback-friendly ride coordination features

This case strengthens clinical evidence that transportation reliability and communication are core drivers of appointment adherence and outcomes.
