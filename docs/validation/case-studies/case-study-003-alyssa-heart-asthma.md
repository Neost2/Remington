# CarePath Case Study #3

## Alyssa (Heart and Asthma Patient)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: Patient
- Medical context: Heart and asthma conditions with specialist and procedure-related appointments
- Transportation context: Relies on insurance transportation for medical visits
- Segment relevance: High-risk patient segment where missed appointments can escalate care risk

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Direct text-message conversation during founder outreach
- Purpose: Validate whether transportation failures are causing missed care
- Prompt context: User was asked whether they had missed appointments due to transportation issues

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quotes from participant:

- "I've actually had to miss my heart appointment and surgery this week because my insurance transportation canceled my ride at 2am."
- "They didn't even show up, or notify me."
- "It happened again."

Observed pattern:

- Last-minute cancelations with no notification
- No reliable backup path on same-day critical visits
- Repeat failures rather than one-off events

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Logistical: Missed multiple same-day appointments and had to manually check status early in the morning
- Medical: Missed heart-related appointment and surgery timing window, increasing clinical risk
- Emotional: Clear frustration and loss of trust due to repeated no-show/cancelation behavior
- System burden: Increased communication burden and avoidable rescheduling loops

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Participant understood the concept quickly once explained in plain language
- Expressed repeated dissatisfaction with current provider options and lack of control
- Signal of demand: Strong need for a system that prevents no-show/cancelation surprises and improves transparency

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation reliability failures are occurring in high-acuity care journeys, not just routine visits
- No-notice or late-notice cancelations are a major root cause of missed care
- Patients want both visibility and agency (status alerts plus ability to choose a dependable provider)
- Messaging that resonated: fixing miscommunication between patient, provider, and transportation

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong

Justification:

- Participant reported specific, recent, high-consequence failures
- Problem was recurring ("It happened again")
- Impact was concrete (missed heart appointment and surgery) and emotionally salient

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

Pilot priorities reinforced by this case:

- Real-time cancelation and delay alerts with confirmed read receipts
- Early warning when a ride is at risk and backup ride escalation workflows
- Appointment criticality flags (for example, surgery and specialty follow-up)
- Option pathway for dependable provider preference where policy allows

This case strengthens the thesis that CarePath must solve both transparency and reliability, especially for high-risk patients.
