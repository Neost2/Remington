# Michelle Bowen - Expanded Case Study

## Pilot Interest + Continuance of Care Mobility (July 2026 Update)

## 1. Updated Scenario Overview

Michelle is a caregiver navigating transportation barriers for her daughter, who uses a non-folding rigid wheelchair.

Current constraints include:

- Finding vehicles that can accommodate a rigid, non-folding wheelchair
- Coordinating airport travel
- Managing mobility at the destination
- Lack of household vehicle access
- Inconsistent rideshare accessibility

Recent outreach confirms Michelle is actively seeking solutions and is highly motivated to participate in a CarePath pilot.

## 2. New Transportation Barrier: Airport Access with Rigid Wheelchair

Michelle attempted to plan a trip to Colorado and encountered a major obstacle: airport access with a wheelchair that cannot fold.

Key issues surfaced:

- Rigid wheelchair incompatibility with common rideshare vehicles
- Airport-bound mobility gaps
- No unified directory for accessible transport options
- Escalating caregiver stress due to fragmented planning paths

This scenario demonstrates the emotional and logistical strain caused by the absence of coordinated accessible transportation.

## 3. Continuance of Care Mobility: The Missing Layer

Even if airport transportation is secured, additional gaps remain across the full care journey:

- Airport arrival transport
- Accessible lodging transport
- Destination-side mobility
- Return-trip coordination

This reinforces the requirement for continuity across the full mobility chain, not just point-to-point transport.

## 4. Michelle's Direct Interest in the CarePath Pilot

Direct participant quote (email):

- "Yes I'm very interested. I want to be able to do fun things or important things with my kids. With no car and a daughter in a wheelchair it's hard to find Ubers, Lyfts etc that can accommodate us."

Validation confirmed:

- High motivation
- Clear unmet need
- Strong alignment with CarePath's mission
- Willingness to participate in early testing

Michelle is a strong candidate for early pilot enrollment once CarePath pilot operations are launched.

## 5. Additional Caregiver Pilot Interest Cluster

Beyond Michelle, additional caregivers have expressed interest in participating in the pilot.

Recurring motivations include:

- Difficulty securing accessible rides
- Inconsistent NEMT availability

This establishes an early-adopter validation cluster (not isolated anecdotes) and reinforces demand for:

- Accessible vehicle matching
- Provider network coordination
- Backup and fallback options
- Continuance of care mobility workflows
