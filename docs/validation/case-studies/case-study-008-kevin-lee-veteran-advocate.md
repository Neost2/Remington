# CarePath Case Study #8

## Kevin Lee (Healthcare Transportation Specialist and Veteran Advocate)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Name: Kevin Lee
- Roles:
  - Healthcare transportation support staff
  - Veteran advocate
  - Owner of an advocacy business that arranges transportation for veterans
- Setting: Clinical environment serving patients with disabilities (TBI, spinal cord injuries, mobility impairments)
- Experience: Daily involvement coordinating, troubleshooting, and personally arranging transportation for vulnerable populations
- Segment relevance: High-value dual-perspective stakeholder (clinic operations plus community advocacy)

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Validation interview (CarePath validation series)
- Selection rationale: Participant operates both inside clinic workflows and outside-clinic veteran transportation advocacy
- Prompt context: Standard stakeholder interview questions on transportation-to-care barriers

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quotes/paraphrased interview evidence:

- "Transportation reliability is the largest barrier to patients arriving on time for appointments."
- "Issues happen almost daily with communication, pickup times, delays, and wrong vehicle types."
- "Transportation should absolutely be part of the care process because patients cannot receive care if they cannot physically arrive."

Core issues identified:

- Transportation reliability failures are routine even when vehicles exist
- Coordination and communication failures create preventable breakdowns
- Vehicle-type mismatches repeatedly fail mobility-impaired patients
- No unified communication channel across caregivers, dispatchers, drivers, clinics, and advocates

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Logistical impact: Missed appointments, delayed arrivals, and cascading schedule disruptions
- Medical impact: Delayed access to time-sensitive and specialty care for high-need patients, including disabled veterans
- Emotional impact: Patient discouragement and reduced motivation after repeated failures
- Workforce impact: Staff burnout due to constant manual rescheduling and troubleshooting
- Financial/operational impact: Ongoing manual coordination overhead and inefficient backup recovery

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Participant position: Strong strategic alignment
- Key belief: Transportation is inseparable from care delivery
- Validation significance: Supports CarePath as a transportation-to-care orchestration layer rather than a standalone ride tool

## 6. Existing Tools and Failure Modes

Current tools used:

- Headsets
- GPS
- Vehicle organization systems
- New tracking system for missed appointments
- Uber Health pilot backup at VA Jackson, MS

Where tools fail:

- Tools are fragmented and do not share visibility
- Legacy process remains heavily manual
- Newer systems add steps without resolving core coordination failure
- Delays still require human intervention and ad hoc escalation

## 7. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation failures are daily, predictable, and often preventable
- Clinics need real-time transportation status and proactive intervention workflows
- Advocates need shared tools to coordinate rides without duplicating clinic effort
- Veterans face unique barriers (distance, accessibility needs, inconsistent provider coverage)
- Advocacy organizations are functioning as unofficial care coordinators due to system gaps

## 8. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong (high-confidence operational signal)

Justification:

- High-frequency issue profile with concrete breakdown categories
- Cross-context evidence from both clinical operations and veteran advocacy work
- Existing workaround experimentation (Uber Health pilot and tracking systems) indicates active demand without a complete solution

## 9. How This Informs the Pilot

Pilot priorities reinforced by this case:

- Build real-time visibility across clinic, dispatch, caregiver, and advocate stakeholders
- Add role-aware communication threads to eliminate fragmented outreach
- Improve vehicle-type matching for accessibility-dependent riders
- Add automated delay detection, recovery, and rescheduling workflows
- Include veteran/advocacy referral pathways as first-class entities in dispatch coordination

This case strongly validates CarePath's core value proposition: unifying transportation coordination, communication, and appointment continuity in one operational layer.
