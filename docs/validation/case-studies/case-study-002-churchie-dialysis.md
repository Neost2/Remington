# CarePath Case Study #2

## Churchie (Dialysis Patient)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: Adult dialysis patient
- Context: Transportation instability and repeated disruption risk for life-sustaining treatment
- Segment relevance: High-risk, high-need patient profile

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Direct outreach conversation
- Selection rationale: Lived experience with dialysis-dependent care and transportation vulnerability
- Prompt focus: Whether transportation issues disrupted attendance to critical treatment

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quote:

- "Car has broke down a few times."

Core issue:

- Recurring transportation failures threaten dialysis attendance.

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Medical: Missed dialysis can increase risk of fluid overload and hospitalization
- Logistical: Limited backup options when transportation fails
- Emotional: Heightened vulnerability and uncertainty around critical care continuity

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

Direct quote:

- "Yes I would use it."

Interpretation:

- Immediate understanding of value and clear intent to adopt.

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation reliability is a recurring pain point with high medical consequences
- Many high-need patients lack dependable backup transport systems
- Simplicity and reliability are critical in this segment

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong

Justification:

- Participant confirmed recurring transport failure and direct care risk
- Participant expressed explicit willingness to use the solution

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

- Prioritize reliability alerts and backup coordination workflows
- Reduce friction for critical recurring care pathways (for example, dialysis)
- Add early intervention features when transportation risk is detected
