# CarePath Case Study #4

## Katina (RN at Arkansas Baptist Hospital)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: Registered Nurse (RN)
- Setting: Arkansas Baptist Hospital, Little Rock
- Context: Direct clinical exposure to patients missing appointments due to transportation barriers
- Segment relevance: Healthcare professional with systemic visibility into patient no-show patterns and root causes

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Facebook Messenger conversation during founder outreach
- Selection rationale: Healthcare worker with firsthand, repeated exposure to transportation-related missed care
- Prompt: Asked about professional experience with patients missing appointments due to transportation

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quote from participant:

- "Some patients miss appointments b/c they don't have reliable transportation. have no gas money or can't afford a Uber or ride share. Sometimes they depend on family members who schedules could change unexpectedly. Then there challenges with public transportation such as limited routes or whatever."

Core issues identified:

- Patients lack financial resources for transportation options (gas, rideshare)
- Unreliable family-based transportation due to schedule conflicts
- Public transportation is inadequate (limited routes, coverage gaps)

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Clinical impact: Patients miss appointments, leading to delayed or fragmented care
- Systemic impact: Healthcare providers experience no-shows and operational inefficiency
- Financial impact: Patients lack funds for transportation costs
- Social impact: Families face unexpected schedule conflicts affecting patient mobility

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Participant engaged constructively with the problem framing
- Clear understanding of the barriers and urgency
- Implied willingness to engage further (continued conversation and detailed response)

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation barriers are a documented, repeated clinical problem, not theoretical
- The issue affects patient populations with limited financial resources
- Family-dependent transportation is common but unreliable
- Public transit gaps are a significant factor
- A healthcare worker's perspective adds credibility and systemic visibility

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong

Justification:

- Direct clinical testimony from a frontline healthcare worker
- Concrete examples of barriers affecting real patients
- Multiple transportation failure modes identified (personal finance, family unreliability, public transit gaps)
- Healthcare professional perspective reinforces problem severity

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

Pilot priorities reinforced by this case:

- Address affordability and payment barriers alongside coordination
- Support family-based transportation planning with better visibility
- Integrate or complement public transit information for coverage areas
- Design for hospital and clinic coordination workflows to capture patient needs
- Consider healthcare-worker referral pathway for identifying patients at transport risk

This case adds professional healthcare-system validation to earlier patient and driver perspectives.
