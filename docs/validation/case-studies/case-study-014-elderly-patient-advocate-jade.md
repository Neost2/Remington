# CarePath Case Study #14

## Jade Harper (Elderly Patient Advocate)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Name: Jade Harper (alias)
- Role: Elderly patient advocate
- Setting: Patient advocacy across older adults, people with disabilities, and medically complex households navigating frequent appointments
- Interview path: Collected by founder's daughter through direct outreach to a trusted community contact
- Segment relevance: Strong access-to-care signal from an advocate perspective spanning patients, families, and clinic workflows

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Structured validation interview (10-question script)
- Interview type: Advocate perspective with cross-stakeholder visibility
- Selection rationale: Captures recurring transportation barriers and explicitly identifies communication fragmentation as the central fix opportunity

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Direct quotes:

- "I believe the biggest challenge is having reliable transportation."
- "For many people, transportation becomes just as much of a barrier as the medical issue itself."
- "The biggest frustration is the lack of communication."

Core issues identified:

- Reliable transportation remains the primary bottleneck for on-time appointment attendance
- Dependence on family, friends, transit, rideshare, or insurance transportation creates fragile handoffs
- Small transportation delays can cause missed appointments that took weeks to schedule
- Current ecosystem is fragmented, with stakeholders operating on different information and timelines

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Patient impact: Missed appointments and delayed treatment or follow-up care
- Family impact: Last-minute schedule disruption and potential work interruption to provide transportation backup
- Clinic impact: Staff time diverted to cancelation handling and rescheduling rather than proactive care support
- Provider impact: Lost appointment capacity when transportation failures produce preventable no-shows
- Equity impact: Seniors, people with disabilities, chronic illness populations, and rural or underserved communities carry disproportionate risk

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Participant strongly supports a connected, real-time coordination model
- "Magic wand" priority: one shared system where patients, caregivers, providers, and transportation services see real-time status, ETA, delays, and updates
- Open to follow-up conversation for additional detail and clarification

## 6. Existing Tools and Failure Modes

- Current tools: family and friend support, Uber/Lyft, public transportation, insurance NEMT providers, and occasional healthcare-organization coordination
- Failure mode #1: tools are not connected, forcing patients to self-coordinate
- Failure mode #2: inconsistent or late communication on delays and cancelations
- Failure mode #3: public transit schedules and affordability constraints do not reliably align with medical appointment needs
- Failure mode #4: transportation coordination quality varies significantly by provider and location

## 7. Key Insights Extracted

- Communication failure is the primary frustration across all stakeholders, not just transportation supply
- A shared, real-time timeline remains the clearest and most repeated design requirement
- Transportation unreliability creates both medical delays and workforce inefficiency inside clinics
- High-need populations are structurally exposed to repeated risk due to frequent appointment demand and low transport resilience
- This interview strengthens the case for CarePath as a coordination layer connecting existing transportation options rather than replacing them

## 8. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong (clear cross-stakeholder framing, specific failure modes, direct alignment with recurring thesis)

Justification:

- Confirms reliability and communication breakdowns as dominant root causes
- Identifies stakeholder-wide burden distribution with concrete operational effects
- Explicitly prioritizes real-time shared visibility as the highest-value intervention
- Names vulnerable segments most affected, reinforcing equity-sensitive product positioning
