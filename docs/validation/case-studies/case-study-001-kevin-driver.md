# CarePath Case Study #1

## Kevin (Transportation Driver)

## 1. Participant Profile

- Role: Professional transportation driver
- Population served: Seniors, Medicaid riders, ambulatory and non-ambulatory patients
- Operating context: Daily routing, wait-time management, dispatch coordination, caregiver communication
- Segment relevance: Frontline transportation stakeholder with direct visibility into systemic care-delivery breakdowns

## 2. Context of the Conversation

- Method: Direct discovery outreach interview
- Purpose: Validate transportation and communication breakdowns affecting appointment completion
- Selection rationale: High-frequency exposure to dispatch, clinic, caregiver, and rider coordination failures

## 3. Problem Identified (User Voice)

Representative quotes and paraphrased observations:

- "If one appointment runs long, it throws off the whole day."
- "Sometimes I take someone to a clinic and the appointment isn't even happening anymore."
- "Caregivers call because they don't know what's going on."

Core problem:

- Appointment and transportation workflows are not synchronized in real time, causing preventable delays and missed care.

## 4. Impact of the Problem

- Logistical: Cascading route delays, wasted trips, excessive back-and-forth calls
- Care delivery: Late or missed appointments across multiple riders
- Emotional: Caregiver frustration and driver stress from being the communication middleman
- Operational: Daily inefficiency from poor visibility into delays, cancelations, and schedule changes

## 5. Reaction to CarePath Concept

- Positive response to transparency-first features
- Highest-value needs identified:
  - Live ETA and pickup windows
  - Automatic alerts for delays and schedule changes
  - Better visibility between dispatch, clinics, caregivers, and riders

## 6. Key Insights Extracted

- Transportation reliability is a major weak link in continuity of care
- Communication fragmentation is a root cause of avoidable disruption
- Non-ambulatory riders need additional planning and should be explicitly prioritized
- A single shared timeline could reduce call volume and stress across stakeholders

## 7. Validation Signal Strength

- Strong

Justification:

- Repeated real-world examples from a frontline operator
- Clear mapping from root causes to operational and care outcomes
- Strong feature-level alignment with the CarePath concept

## 8. How This Informs the Pilot

- Prioritize live ETA, delay, and cancelation visibility
- Add earlier-warning logic to reduce ripple-effect delays
- Include workflow support for non-ambulatory patients requiring special handling
- Focus on reducing preventable wasted trips and communication overhead
