# Standard Stakeholder Interview Questions

Use these same 10 questions for each stakeholder interview to keep your data cleaner, more comparable, and focused.

1. What is the biggest challenge you see when it comes to people getting to medical appointments on time?
2. How often do you see transportation issues causing missed or delayed appointments?
3. When transportation falls through, what usually happens next?
4. Who ends up carrying the burden when a ride does not show: the patient, staff, family, or someone else?
5. What is the most frustrating part of the current process for you personally?
6. What tools, systems, or services are people using today to coordinate rides?
7. Where do those tools or systems fall short?
8. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one part of the transportation-to-care process, what would you change first?
9. Who else do you think feels this pain the most, and why?
10. Would you be open to a quick follow-up conversation if I need to clarify anything you shared?

## Optional Interviewer Notes

- Ask in order first, then use follow-up prompts only when needed.
- Keep wording consistent across interviews unless clarity requires a small adjustment.
- Capture at least one direct quote tied to question 1, 5, and 8.
