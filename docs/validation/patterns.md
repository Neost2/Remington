# CarePath Validation Patterns

## 1. Transportation failure patterns

### 1.1 Recurring failure modes

- **Pattern:** Transportation failures repeat across roles and settings, with the same core issues showing up in patient, caregiver, driver, and RN conversations.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-001-kevin-driver.md`, `case-study-002-churchie-dialysis.md`, `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `driver`, `patient`, `rn`, `no-coordination`, `stress`, `no-backup`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "If one appointment runs long, it throws off the whole day."
- "Yes. Throughout my healthcare experience, I have seen patients miss appointments due to transportation barriers."

### 1.2 Last-minute and no-notice failures

- **Pattern:** Cancellations and schedule changes often arrive too late to recover, creating missed care and wasted trips.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-001-kevin-driver.md`, `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`
- **Tags:** `last-minute-cancelation`, `no-notification`, `wasted-trip`, `clinic-no-visibility`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "I've actually had to miss my heart appointment and surgery this week because my insurance transportation canceled my ride at 2am."
- "Sometimes I take someone to a clinic and the appointment isn't even happening anymore."

### 1.3 No backup options

- **Pattern:** When the first transport plan fails, many participants have no dependable fallback.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-002-churchie-dialysis.md`, `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `no-backup`, `insurance-transportation`, `family-unreliable`, `no-risk-detection`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Car has broke down a few times."
- "They didn't even show up, or notify me."

## 2. Clinical impact patterns

### 2.1 Missed critical care (dialysis, heart, surgery)

- **Pattern:** Transportation failure directly threatens time-sensitive care such as dialysis, surgery, and specialty follow-ups.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-002-churchie-dialysis.md`, `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `dialysis`, `heart`, `surgery`, `missed-appointment`, `missed-surgery`, `hospitalization-risk`, `high-risk`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "I've actually had to miss my heart appointment and surgery this week because my insurance transportation canceled my ride at 2am."
- "Transportation can be a significant barrier to accessing both primary and specialty care."

### 2.2 Disrupted medication and follow-up

- **Pattern:** Missed rides do not just affect the appointment itself; they disrupt medication management and follow-up continuity.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `medication-disruption`, `delayed-care`, `chronic`, `follow-up`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Transportation challenges often lead to delayed treatment missed follow-up appointments medication management issues and poorer health outcomes."

## 3. Operational and system patterns

### 3.1 Wasted trips and route disruption

- **Pattern:** One delayed ride or no-show can throw off an entire route and waste multiple trips.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-001-kevin-driver.md`, `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`
- **Tags:** `wasted-trip`, `late-pickup`, `route-disruption`, `schedule-chaos`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "If one appointment runs long, it throws off the whole day."

### 3.2 Clinic no-shows and schedule chaos

- **Pattern:** Clinics and transportation systems lose alignment, leading to no-shows and unpredictable schedule churn.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-001-kevin-driver.md`, `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `clinic-no-visibility`, `no-coordination`, `no-shared-timeline`, `missed-appointment`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Sometimes I take someone to a clinic and the appointment isn't even happening anymore."
- "Some patients miss appointments b/c they don't have reliable transportation."

### 3.3 Communication black holes

- **Pattern:** People involved in the ride rarely share a single source of truth, so everyone ends up calling around for answers.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-001-kevin-driver.md`, `case-study-003-alyssa-heart-asthma.md`, `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `no-notification`, `no-shared-timeline`, `communication-gap`, `no-coordination`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Caregivers call because they don't know what's going on."
- "They didn't even show up, or notify me."

## 4. Socioeconomic and access patterns

### 4.1 Financial barriers (gas, rideshare, etc.)

- **Pattern:** Cost alone can make transportation inaccessible, even when a ride technically exists.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `no-gas`, `rideshare-unaffordable`, `insurance-transportation`, `financial-barrier`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "...they don't have reliable transportation. have no gas money or can't afford a Uber or ride share."

### 4.2 Family-dependent but unreliable transportation

- **Pattern:** Families and friends often help, but shifting schedules make them unreliable as the only backup.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `family-unreliable`, `no-backup`, `no-coordination`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Sometimes they depend on family members who schedules could change unexpectedly."

### 4.3 Public transit gaps

- **Pattern:** Public transit coverage is not dependable enough to serve as the sole transport solution in all markets.
- **Seen in:** `case-study-004-katina-rn.md`, `case-study-005-elijah-rn-bsn.md`
- **Tags:** `public-transit-gap`, `rural`, `access-gap`
- **Example quote(s):**
- "Then there challenges with public transportation such as limited routes or whatever."
